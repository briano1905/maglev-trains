What is Maglev?

Magnetic Levitation occurs when the magnetic force counters the force of gravity

Maglev Trains

Maglev train in Shanghai, China
  • uses magnets to levitate
  • uses magnets to accelerate/decelerate

Pros

  • Extremely fast speed upto 600km/hr or $1.67*10^5ms^{-1}$
  • Smooth and quite operation
  • Uses less energy
  • Easy to maintain
  • Safer than conventional trains

Cons

  • Expensive tracks compared to the railroad tracks
  • Not compatible with railroad track, and therefore require the whole route to be rebuild

How does it work?

Electromagnet

Superconductor

Meissner Effect

Electromagnetic suspension (EMS)

  • levitation magnets forces the train up countering the force of gravity
  • guidance magnet is constantly altered using a feedback loop to stabilize the train

Electrodynamic suspension (EDS)

  • uses repulsive magnets to lift the train
  • and figure 8 guidance magnets to support and stabilize the cabin
  • stable by the nature of repulsive forces

Electromagnetic propulsion (EMP)

  • alternating current to switch the pole of the electromagnet
  • which creates a magnetic forces to accelerate/decelerate the train
